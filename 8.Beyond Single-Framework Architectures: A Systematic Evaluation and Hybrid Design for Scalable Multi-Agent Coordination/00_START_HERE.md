# Hey Ayesha — Read This First

## What is this paper about?

This paper compares three popular AI frameworks — CrewAI, AutoGen, and LangChain — that are used to coordinate teams of AI agents working together on complex tasks. The researchers tested all three on a realistic wildfire emergency simulation (CREW-WILDFIRE) involving 100+ agents, maps with over 1 million cells, and 17 different task levels. After identifying the strengths and weaknesses of each framework, they designed a brand-new **hybrid system** combining the best parts of LangGraph and CrewAI, which achieved a 96.1% success rate while using 76.2% fewer computational resources than any single framework alone.

## Why does it matter?

As AI agents become more common in real-world applications — emergency response, logistics, healthcare, robotics — choosing the wrong coordination framework can mean wasted money, slow decisions, or mission failure. This paper gives the first rigorous, evidence-based comparison of these frameworks at realistic scale, and proves that a smart hybrid design can be both highly capable and cost-efficient, making large-scale AI coordination practically deployable.
