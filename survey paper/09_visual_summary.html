📖 The Core Story

PROBLEM Traditional control methods can't handle unpredictable real-world conditions in autonomous systems.
SOLUTION A systematic review of DRL across 4 domains, with structured comparison tables to map the field.
KEY RESULT DRL consistently outperforms traditional methods. VILDS: 98.2% lane accuracy. SIFRCNN: 23% miss rate drop. SAC: 100% robot safety.
SO WHAT DRL is already in Tesla, Waymo, Amazon & DJI — but Sim-to-Real gap and safety certification remain unsolved.

⚙️ DRL Algorithms — Which Does What

DDPG
Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient
Best for continuous control (steering, speed). Most-used algorithm in the survey.
DQN
Deep Q-Network
Best for discrete decisions (routing, braking events). Landmark 2013 invention by DeepMind.
SAC
Soft Actor-Critic
Safest algorithm — adds exploration bonus. Achieved 100% safety in robot arm planning.
PPO
Proximal Policy Optimization
Stable, incremental learning. Used for robotic swarms and AWS DeepRacer racing.
D3QN
Dueling Double DQN
Improved DQN. Reduces overestimation. Used for drone interception & obstacle avoidance.
MARL
Multi-Agent RL
Multiple agents coordinating. Used for drone fleet pollution monitoring.

⚠️ Open Challenges

🔬
Sim-to-Real Gap
Systems trained in simulation underperform in real-world deployment. #1 unsolved problem.
🛡️
Safety & Robustness
DRL systems can fail on rare edge cases. Safety certification frameworks don't exist yet.
📈
Scalability
DRL often can't generalize across diverse, unstructured real-world scenarios.
⚖️
Ethics & Regulation
Liability, privacy, bias, and global regulatory frameworks are all unresolved.

💬 Quick Q&A — If Sir Asks...

What is the main contribution?
First unified survey covering all 4 autonomous system domains with structured comparison tables — not just a paper list.
What is the biggest limitation?
Sim-to-Real transfer remains unsolved — systems work great in simulation but lose performance in the real world.
What is the future direction?
Integrating Large Language Models (LLMs) and transformer architectures for smarter, more generalizable autonomous systems.
Why DRL over traditional methods?
Traditional methods follow fixed rules — they break in unexpected situations. DRL learns and adapts, making it far more robust.
