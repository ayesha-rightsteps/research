# Hey Ayesha — Read This First

## What is this paper about?
This paper tackles the challenge of controlling a swarm of drones (UAVs) that must cover multiple target areas while avoiding obstacles and evading a pursuing enemy — all without a central controller telling each drone what to do. The authors propose RALLY, a system that gives each drone the ability to reason in natural language using a Large Language Model (LLM), while also learning from experience through Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL). The result is a smarter swarm where drones dynamically switch roles — Commander, Coordinator, or Executor — to collaborate, reach consensus on where to fly, and complete their mission more effectively than any existing approach.

## Why does it matter?
Drone swarms are increasingly being used for disaster response, surveillance, and military operations, but coordinating them intelligently under real-world adversarial conditions remains an unsolved problem. RALLY bridges the gap between the interpretability and reasoning power of language models and the adaptability of reinforcement learning, opening the door to truly autonomous, scalable multi-UAV systems deployable on resource-limited onboard hardware.
